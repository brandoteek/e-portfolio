Security and Risk Management

Security and Risk Management Module

Learning Outcomes

- Discuss the difference between qualitative and quantitative risk assessments.
- Describe how to carry out both kinds of assessments.
- Explain what is meant by and how to create threat models.
- Demonstrate how to create quantitative risk models.
- Discuss and design DR solutions.

UNIT 7 - Introduction to the Concepts of Quantitative Risk Modelling

- Discuss what is meant by quantitative risk modelling (QRM).
- Review different approaches to QRM including Monte Carlo Simulations and Bayes theoretical models.
- Discuss the principles and antipatterns for each approach.

WIKI - Keywords:
- 2 risk approaches: Qualitative and Quantitative.
- Qualitative: Risk Matrices.
- Quantitative: Monte Carlo Simulation | Bayes Theorem | Multi-Criteria Decision Analysis (MCDA)
- Monte Carlo simulation: domain of possible inputs | generation of inputs randomly by using a Probability distribution over the domain | Deterministic computation.
- Anti patterns of Monte Carlo: Tool to run the simulation | Sampling method | Non-standard distributions | Demanding computational resources.
- Bayes Theorem: based on the formula P(A|B) = p(A)*p (B|A) / p(B); where P(A|B): probability of A given B is true - viceversea for P(B|A). P(x): probability of "x".
- Bayes principles: Subjective Probabilities | hypothesis.
- Anti patterns of Bayes Theorem: Ambiguity ( not all sources can be quantified) | Ignorance ( Arbitrary answers) | Ellsberg Paradox ( preferable calculated risks)
- Decision Problem: Choice | Sorting | Ranking | Description | Elimination | Design | Elicitation.
- MCDA Principles: Type of Decisions | Possible techniques based on the calculation of type of decisions. - Anti patterns of MCDA : Incorrect Selection | Rank reversal | Accuracy. - Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP).
- Analytic Network Process ( ANP).
- Technique for Order Performance by Similarity to Ideal solution (TOPSIS).

UNIT 8 - Implementing Quantitative Risk Models

- Discuss how QRM can be applied to real world problems.
- Review case studies of how QRM techniques are used.
- Provide a critical evaluation of some of the techniques available.

WIKI - Keywords:
- It was used a Monte Carlo simulation scenario for an Organization that will deploy digitalisation and supply chain processes.
With the Monte Carlo simulation it was possible to generate a high number of simulation and analyse the outcome by modifying the Probability distribution ( Normal distribution) and the values of the mean, standard deviation and values of the costs associated ( IT Infrastructure costs, Payroll costs, marketing costs, Income) etc.
- With a QRM approach it was possible to obtain that with more accurate data, its possible to get precise results with different usages for an Organization. However, it would require considerable computational resources and more exhaustive data evaluation and the association between the parameters that involve costs and incomes.

UNIT 9 - Risk, Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

- Review how and why to create business continuity (BC) and disaster recovery (DR) plans.
- Discuss the main determining factors in BC/DR plans – including Business Impact Assessments (BIA), Recovery Time Objectives (RTOs) and Recovery Point Objectives (RPOs).
- Discuss emerging trends in Information Risk Management.

WIKI - Keywords:
- TOGAF (The Open Group Architecture Framework ) used together with the SABSA ( Sherwood Applied Business Security Architecture).
- ADM ( Architecture Development Method).
- Business Continuity(BC): Risk Assessment | Business Impact Analysis (BIA) | BC Strategy | Policies, Procedures | Regular tests and evaluation.
- Disaster Recovery(DR): Business based on IT Infrastructure, operational when BC is required.
- DR Plan and Design factors: RTO ( Recovery Time objective - how quickly the recovery) | RPO ( Recovery Point Objective - from what points the recovery needs to happen).
- Digital Transformation Maturity: It has 6 Stages.
- Single vs Multiple Suppliers based on High Availability, Redundancy and business criticality.
- Cloudification: Cloud computing adoption. Private vs Hybrid cloud.
- Share Responsibility Model ( IaaS, PaaS, SaaS, On-premise ).
- DRaaS: Disaster Recovery as a Service.
- PITR: Point in Time Recovery/Restore. ( Rollback).
